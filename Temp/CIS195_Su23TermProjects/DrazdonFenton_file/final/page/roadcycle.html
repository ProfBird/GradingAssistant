A brief history of the Military Police Corp

A look at the lower enlisted and their roles in the great military machine.

The road cycle is the bread and butter of the military police corp, where units within a battalian take monthly turns patrolling on one of three eight hour shifts around their base. this includes pulling traffic, responding to calls, and a lot of meeting up with your squad in remote places to smoke and share stories on shift. A lot of my own time patrolling was providing a presence at busy intersections to dissuade people from speeding through areas or at one of the gates turning away people not authorized to be on base. By far one of the best patrols to get put on was about an hour away at one of the MWR (Morale, wellfare, and relaxation) sites at the beach. a long federally owned stretch of beach and cabins, that yes people still needed federal ID to enter.

"Assist, Protect, Defend"
The United States Army's Military Police provide an important function in the full spectrum of Army operations as a member of the Maneuver, Fires, and Effects division. The Military Police Corps provides expertise in policing, detainment, and stability operations in order to enhance security and enable mobility. Military Police are actively utilized in direct combat and during peacetime.
