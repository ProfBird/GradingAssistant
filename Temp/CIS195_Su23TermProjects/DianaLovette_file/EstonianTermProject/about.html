About Estonia and Estonian

Where in the World is Estonia?

Estonia is a small and beautiful country in Northern Europe. It's surrounded by the Gulf of Finland to the north and the Baltic Sea to the west. It shares borders with Latvia to the south and Russia to the east.

Tallinn is Estonia's capital city, which is known for its medieval old town and lively culture full of museums, cafes and beautiful promenades.

About the Language

Estonian is the official language of Estonia. It belongs to the Finno-Ugric language family, which is quite unique and different from most other European Languages.

Estonian has 27 letters in its alphabet, including special characters like "Õ" and "Ü", which give it a distinct sound.

Many people find Estonian challenging to learn due to its complex grammar, however, as it is a language that Estonians take great pride in, it plays a crucial role in preserving their cultural identity and heritage.

Interesting Facts

  • Only about 921,369 people in the world speak Estonian natively.

  • Although the official language of Estonia is Estonian, many people in Estonia speak English, Russian, Finnish and German.

  • The population of Estonia is only about 1.3 million.

  • Estonia is a digital society. Residents can do nearly everything they need to online including paying their taxes, getting a medical prescription, registering a business or voting.

  • Estonia has over 2000 islands, many with their own unique cultures.

  • More than half of the country is covered by forest land.
