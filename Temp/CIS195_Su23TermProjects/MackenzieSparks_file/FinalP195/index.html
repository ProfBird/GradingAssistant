Sunflowers

The Flower That Makes Everyone Happy




My Garden

Every year I like to plant an assortment of flowers. Nothing crazy, just a happy snack for the neighborhood bees and butterflies. Come springtime, I am out in my yard with bags of soil and packets of seeds. And every year, my wonderful neighbor shares some of her sunflowers-starts. This year I thought I would change it up and add some different varieties of sunflower seeds so the ones I was gifted are not alone.

Why Sunflowers Are So Loved

"Sunflowers symbolise loyalty, adoration thanks to the myth of Clytie and Apollo. And, because of their association with the sun, sunflowers are well-known for being a happy flower and the perfect bloom for a summer flower delivery to brighten someone's mood! But, they actually have different meanings across cultures. In China, sunflowers symbolise long life, vitality and good luck. And to Native Americans sunflowers symbolised harvest and provision. Basically no matter where you are, sunflowers are a positive flower that brings joy to many!"-Bloom and Wild Website


Fun Facts About Sunflowers:

  • The tallest recorded sunflower was a ginormous 9.17m (30 ft 1 inch)
  • The sunflower is now the national flower for Russia and Ukraine
  • Sunflowers are usually given on a third wedding anniversary.
  • Each sunflower can produce between 1000-2000 seeds!
  • They are native to the Americas, brought to the rest of the world by Spanish Conquistadors around 1500
  • Sunflowers plants display a behavior called heliotropism, where the blooms follow the path of the sun every day
  • In 2012 sunflowers made their first trip space with U.S. astronaut Don Pettit, who brought along a few companions to the International Space Station: sunflower seeds
