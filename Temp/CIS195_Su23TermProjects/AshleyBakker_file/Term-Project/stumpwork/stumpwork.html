Stumpwork Embroidery

A lot of stitches used in surface embroidery are used in stumpwork embroidery, specifically the ones that add a 3D effect. Stumpwork embroidery, also called raised embroidery, uses these stitches along with other techniques to raise the design on top of the surface. Some techniques include using padding/stuffing like foam or felt, using wires or beads to structure the threads, or even cutting out fabric with it's own embroidered design and applying it to the main piece, called slips. Any method that allows for thread to pop off its surface is welcomed.

Materials

These materials are similar to surface embroidery, refer to Surface Embroidery for specifics on general materials.
  • Needle: A standard embroidery/crewel needle.
  • Fabric: Anything that can hold the stitches and weight of extra padding/wires.
  • Hoop: Just like with surface embroidery, you will need a hoop to hold the fabric tight.
  • Thread: Embroidery floss.
  • Extras:
    • Scissors to cut the thread.
    • Padding like felt, foam, stuffing or wool for under large threaded areas.
    • Wire to support thinner forms and provide structure.
    • Fabric or canvas to be embroidered and cut out for slips.
    • Embellishments like pearls, beads, sequins or buttons to be used as is or to be wrapped in thread.
